# Possible Venues — Citation Hallucination Detector Paper

Working list of submission venues, ordered by fit. Update as decisions
are made.

---

## Tier A — Strongest fits

### First Monday
- **URL:** https://firstmonday.org
- **Why it fits:** Tolerates personal voice, political framing about
  commercial AI, and the epistemological argument. Open access, no
  APCs, broad readership. Peer-reviewed but practitioner-friendly.
- **Tailoring needed:** Minimal. The paper's current voice and
  argument are already First-Monday-shaped.
- **Friction:** None major.

### Research Integrity and Peer Review (BMC)
- **URL:** https://researchintegrityjournal.biomedcentral.com
- **Why it fits:** Most directly aligned subject matter — citation
  fabrication, scholarly integrity, peer review. The paper's central
  finding (epistemological error in detection systems) is exactly
  the kind of contribution this journal publishes.
- **Tailoring needed:** Tighter clinical framing. The "Whose Writing
  Is This, Anyway?" tone would need to read more measured.
- **Friction:** APCs (BMC is OA but charges). Confirm fee structure
  before committing. Tier 3 partial external validation
  (CiteAudit subset) is probably required at this venue.

### Journal of Electronic Publishing
- **URL:** https://journals.publishing.umich.edu/jep/
- **Why it fits:** Open-access scholarship venue, comfortable with
  tool-and-method papers, no APCs, practitioner audience.
- **Tailoring needed:** Light. May want a slightly more concrete
  "what editors should do with this" section.
- **Friction:** Lower visibility than First Monday or JASIST.

## Tier B — Plausible fits

### Code4Lib Journal
- **URL:** https://journal.code4lib.org
- **Why it fits:** Open-source ethos, free-public-API stack,
  practitioner-tool framing. Open peer review, no APCs. First-person
  voice tolerated.
- **Tailoring needed:** Add ~1 paragraph reframing use cases for
  library workflows — reference desk verification, ILL,
  collection-integrity checking, Zotero/EBSCO integration angles.
  The current "small journal editors and educators" framing won't
  land as well with librarian readers.
- **Friction:** Currently in a special-issue cycle ("other papers
  will not be read for several months" per their submissions page).
  Real timing delay.

### Journal of the Association for Information Science and Technology (JASIST)
- **URL:** https://asistdl.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/23301643
- **Why it fits:** Highest-prestige venue in this list. Citation
  integrity and information-retrieval evaluation are core topics.
- **Tailoring needed:** Substantial. Strip personal voice in favor
  of clinical framing. Tighten methodology section. Add formal
  evaluation against external benchmarks (Tier 3 work). The
  political framing about Big GenAI would need to come out or be
  reframed as objective comparison.
- **Friction:** Slow review cycle. Tier 3 external validation
  (CiteAudit, Rao benchmark) is effectively required. Wiley APCs.

## Current recommendation

If submitting **without** Tier 3 external validation: **First Monday**
or **Journal of Electronic Publishing**. Both accept the paper roughly
as it stands.

If willing to invest 1–2 days in CiteAudit subset validation:
**Research Integrity and Peer Review** becomes the best subject-matter
match.

**JASIST** only if the personal voice is fully stripped and the political
framing is removed — that's a different paper, not this one.
